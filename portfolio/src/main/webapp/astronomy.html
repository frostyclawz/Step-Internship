Gravitational Lensing and Black Holes

Black Holes are by far the most weird phenomena that are present within our universe. These objects are points where gravity is so strong that the object's escape velocity is greater than the speed of light (often denoted as c which is 300,000,000 m/s). Black holes are formed from the death of very heavy stars between 2 to 3 times the mass of the Sun. Black holes are often associated with the movement of time, as the gravitational influence of these objects effectively slow time down to a crawl. These objects are effectively invisible on all bands of the electromagnetic spctrum and can be detected by a few means:

  • Gravitaional Lensing
  • Gravitaional disturbances of objects around the black hole
  • X-ray/Gamma Ray emmissions from ejected matter
  • Etc...

VY Canis Majoris: One Huge Star

VY Canis Majoris is one of the largest stars that humanity has ever discovered. With a radius of 613.85 million milesm(around 1,419 times larger than our star, the Sun.) and a mass of around 17 solar masses (17 times "heavier" than the Sun). This star is a red hypergiant, one of the largest classes of red supergiants known to astronomers today. This star is absolutely gigantic in size. If it were superimposed atop the Sun, it would extend out to around the orbit of Saturn, give-or-take! Luckily this star is around 3,960 light years (1.2 kiloparsecs) away!

Neutron Stars

Neutron stars are the second stellar corpses that I am talking about here. Neutron stars are generated when heavier stars die in supernova explosions of around 1.4 and up times the mass of the sun. Neutron stars are odd because once formed, these objects spin at very high speeds, on the order of thousands of times a second.
